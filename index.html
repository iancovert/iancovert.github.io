Ian C. Covert

I'm a Ph.D. student in the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering at the University of Washington, where I'm fortunate to be advised by Su-In Lee. I also collaborate with Scott Lundberg from Microsoft Research and Uygar Sümbül from the Allen Institute for Brain Science.

Previously, I worked with Emily Fox and was a student researcher at Google Brain working with Jiening Zhan and Ming Jack Po. Before that, I completed my Bachelor's program at Columbia University, where I did computational neuroscience research in Liam Paninski's group.

My research interests touch on deep learning, explainable AI and representation learning. I spent a couple years of my PhD working on making ML models more transparent, in particular fleshing out the theory behind interpretability methods and making Shapley values more efficient.

I co-instructed an explainable AI course with my advisor, Su-In Lee, in Spring 2022. Our course materials are available here.
