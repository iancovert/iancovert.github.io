Research

My main research interest is creating explainable AI tools for black-box machine learning models. Besides developing theory and algorithms for these methods, I also work on applying them to problems in biology and medicine.

Some of my recent projects include:

  • FastSHAP, an amortized approach for producing Shapley value estimates in real-time [paper] [code]
  • SAGE, a principled approach to calculating global feature importance using Shapley values [paper] [code] [blog]
  • Removal-based explanations, a new class of methods that unifies 25+ model explanation approaches (including SHAP, LIME, Meaningful Perturbations, L2X, etc.) [paper] [code]
  • Improving KernelSHAP by developing methods for accelerating the estimation process, automatically detecting its convergence, and providing confidence intervals for the results [paper] [code] [blog]
